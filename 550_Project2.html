1) DESCRIPTION OF THE DATASET¶

The dataset gives suicide rates overview from 1986 to 2016. We are are using a subset of the dataframe from analysis ther statistics from 1996 to 2016. The dataset has a total of 12 columns

  • Country : Lists the countries where the suicide rates have been recorded

  • Year : Each record of suicide recorded in the year from 1986 ti 2016.

  • Sex : Denotes the gender of the person - Male or female

  • AGE : The age is binned into 6 levels : '5-14 years', '15-24 years', '25-34 years', '35-54 years', '55-74 years', '75+years

  • Suicide_no : Number of suicides recorded for a particular category of age bin, sex and year

  • Population : Population of the country at the time of record taken

  • suicides/100k pop : Proportion of suicides per 100,000 of the population

  • country- year : Concated information of country and year

  • HDI for year: Human Development Index for that particular year. A composite index measuring average achievement in three basic dimensions of human development—a long and healthy life, knowledge and a decent standard of living

  • gdp_for_year : Gross Domestic Product for the year recorded

  • gdp_per_capita: Gross Domestic Product per capita of the population

  • generation : Recorded of the generations - 6 levles - G.I. Generation', 'Generation X', 'Boomers', 'Silent', 'Millenials', 'Generation Z

It is satisfying to note that the proportion of suicides has fallen steadily over time! We see the suicide rates suddenly drop in 2014 to 2016.

There seems to be a bit of an increasing trend in 1997 - 1998 in all age groups but the 5 -14 years. We are lucky to see no suicides in the 5 - 14 years category. After 1998 we observe a general downward trend up until 2015. There is sudden decrease from 2015 to 2016 and no recorded suicides in in the 5 - 14 years category.

The most alarming category that needs to looked into is the age group of 35-45 years. There seems to be a sudden increase from 1998 to 2004. This is a distributing statistics as we lost a significant number of the mid-life age group. This potentially can be attributed to the famines and recessions that the world for turmoiled into. Loosing this age group of the population puts an increasing pressure on the world economy to bridge the gap between the young workforce and driving workforce of the economic statuses in the country.

One of the disturbing category of age group that can be looked at is the 75+ years. There are by far the highest in proportion of suicides. This is bare truth, yet painful. We have a significant portion of the world's elderly population. The reasons can be enormous, old age, health, retirement homes. Whatever be the reason death by suicide after a long hard life is unjust and we have to address this to get some wise experience in the younger population too!

7) RESEARCH QUESTIONS¶

Question 1

When analyzing the top countries with highest suicide numbers 2015, we see a negative or no correlation with GDP_per_capita and suicide numbers. It is common to relate GDP growth with high income and money matters. This leads to various socio-economic problems. However, that has not proven to be the case here and we have clashing GDP data among the top 5 countries. We can research future into the other reasons that are contributing to the suicides and arrive at a deeper understanding how improving a country's economic standing could help prevent the suicide numbers.

Question 2

When analysing the graphs we found that women generally tend to be more stronger mentally than men as their suicide rates substantially less as compared to men across various generations. However this data alone is not substantial to understand if this claim is true. We might need more psycological data rel.ated to why they are involved in such tragic decisions. Also this difference in male and female suicide rates are constant and consistent among all the different generations. Hence there should be an underlying reason why men tend to be more sensitive consistently among all generations (through facing the same war and economic depressions like men) while they are seen as the stronger sex.This can be supported by the fact that men at that generation were considered more responsible to bring food to the family and save the nation while women stayed home and took care of household. However we cannot come to a complete concreate conclusion about this and would require more information to support our claim.

8)SUMMARY AND CONCLUSIONS¶

Suicide statistics is probably an untouched area in the World Economic forum, but these statistics contribute to the future trend of the population of countries. They can help determine the proportion of population in different ages that gives sense of what the respective Governments can anticipate while planning their economic trends and investments. It can can provide insights on areas of improvement, specifically alluding to the 35 - 45 age group that we analyzed in the graphs above. Loosing that chunk of population can be put he economy out of balance. In reference to the top countries in 2015 with highest suicide numbers, United States tops the list followed by the Russian Federation. It is ironic to say how dictatorship beats democracy in determining the mental state of the population.From our analysis we also see suicide rates higher among males than females and the highest number of suicides occurred among the GI Generation. A sense of this data tells us where exactly in those 20 years did we lose a people and also throws some light on the time period. The distribution of population faceted by generations also gives us an idea of what we are looking at.
